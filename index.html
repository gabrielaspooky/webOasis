Behind the hearts

Oasis was a British rock band formed in Manchester in 1991. The band was originally composed of Liam Gallagher (vocals), Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs (guitar), Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan (bass), and Tony McCarroll (drums), with Liam's older brother Noel Gallagher joining later as the lead guitarist and primary songwriter.

Known for their hit albums "Definitely Maybe" (1994) and "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" (1995), Oasis became one of the leading bands of the Britpop movement, achieving worldwide fame. Their anthemic songs like "Wonderwall" and "Don't Look Back in Anger" defined a generation. Despite frequent internal conflicts and lineup changes, Oasis continued to release successful music until their breakup in 2009, leaving a lasting legacy in rock history.
